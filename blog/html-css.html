What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

In CSS, the term "container model" is used when talking about design and layout. The CSS box model is essentially a contatiner for every HTML element. It consists of: content, padding, borders and margins. The image below illustrates the container model:
Content - The innermost part of the container. Content could be an image, text, heading, etc.
Padding - The inner space between the content and the border of your container.
Border - The perimeter of the box. Borders can be invisible or coloured
Margin - The outer space surrounding the container.
